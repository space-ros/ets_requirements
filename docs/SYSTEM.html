1.0 SYSTEM001

The ETS shall store events that occur in the spacecraft.

2 SYSTEM002

The ETS shall retain events until those events are marked for deletion.

3 SYSTEM003

The ETS shall have a facility for triggering the reporting of retained events.

4 SYSTEM004

The ETS shall support once-off events.

5 SYSTEM005

The ETS shall support regularly-occurring events.

6 SYSTEM006

An event shall be uniquely identifiable.

7 SYSTEM007

An event shall include a type.

8 SYSTEM008

An event shall include a priority.

9 SYSTEM009

An event shall include a value.

10 SYSTEM010

An event shall include an origin.

11 SYSTEM011

An event may include additional data.

12 SYSTEM012

An event value may include a human-readable textual representation.

13 SYSTEM014

The ETS shall report events to external entities in accordance with registered reporting policies

14 SYSTEM015

The ETS shall be able to report an occurred event to a subset of external entities in accordance with registered reporting policies.

15 SYSTEM016

The ETS shall be able to provide events to the flight software.

16 SYSTEM017

The ETS shall provide events to external entities as close to simultaneously as is practicable.

17 SYSTEM018

Event data shall be robust to corruption.

18 SYSTEM019

The ETS shall retain all events up to a fixed limit in accordance with the registered retention policies.

19 SYSTEM020

The ETS shall allow specification of event retention policies.

20 SYSTEM021

The ETS shall have a facility for specifying event retention policies at development time.

21 SYSTEM022

The specification of event retention policies shall be possible at runtime without requiring source code changes.

22 SYSTEM023

An event retention policy shall rely on event type, event priority, event identifier, and event frequency over a given time window.

23 SYSTEM024

The ETS shall allow specification of event reporting policies.

24 SYSTEM025

The ETS shall allow different reporting policies per event consumer.

25 SYSTEM026

The ETS shall have a facility for specifying the event reporting policies at development time.

26 SYSTEM027

The specification of event reporting policies shall be possible at runtime without requiring source code changes.

27 SYSTEM028

An event reporting policy shall rely on one or more of event type, event priority, event identifier, and event frequency over a given time window.

28 SYSTEM029

The ETS shall allow for events of different types.

29 SYSTEM030

The ETS shall place a fixed limit on the number of event types available.

30 SYSTEM031

The ETS shall allow for events of different priorities.

31 SYSTEM032

The ETS shall have a fixed set of priority levels available.

32 SYSTEM033

The ETS shall have a facility for specifying the type of an event when specifying the event.

33 SYSTEM034

The ETS shall have a facility for specifying the priority of an event when specifying the event.

34 SYSTEM035

When the limit on retained events is reached, the ETS shall automatically determine which events to discard and which to retain based on event retention policies available at the time.

35 SYSTEM036

The ETS shall have a facility for specifying, at development time, the resource limits for retaining events.

36 SYSTEM037

The ETS shall create an event when resource limits for retaining events are about to be exceeded.

37 SYSTEM038

The resource-limits-exceeded event shall have the highest priority.

38 SYSTEM039

The ETS shall allow the reporting of retained events.

39 SYSTEM040

The ETS shall allow the reporting of individual retained events to be triggered manually by an event consumer.

40 SYSTEM041

When communications with the ground are possible, the ETS shall allow the reporting/downlinking of events as they occur in accordance with registered reporting policies.

41 SYSTEM042

When a downlink session commences, the ETS shall send events in order according to the registered reporting policies.

42 SYSTEM043

The ETS shall have the capability to generate events itself.

43 SYSTEM044

When a retained event that has not been reported in accordance with a registered reporting policy is removed, the ETS shall generate an event.

44 SYSTEM045

The ETS shall not be restricted to reporting events in order of occurrence.

45 SYSTEM046

The ETS shall have the capability of reporting statistical summaries of event occurrences as an alternative to the events themselves to event consumers.

46 SYSTEM047

The ETS shall have the capability of reporting statistical summaries of event occurrences in addition to the events themselves to event consumers.

47 SYSTEM048

The ETS shall have a facility for specifying the time window over which to calculate the trend for an event value.

48 SYSTEM049

When an event value is outside its trend, the ETS shall create an event.

49 SYSTEM050

The ETS shall have the capability of retaining events that occur during platform startup.

50 SYSTEM051

The ETS shall have the capability of retaining events that occur during application startup.

51 SYSTEM052

The ETS shall be capable of retaining events that occur when the spacecraft is in an unknown state.

52 SYSTEM053

The ETS shall keep all retained events across a full restart of the spacecraft and flight software.

53 SYSTEM054

The ETS shall not require a middleware for providing events to event consumers.

54 SYSTEM055

The ETS shall be capable of using a middleware for providing events to event consumers.

55 SYSTEM056

The ETS shall have a facility for manually causing an event to occur.

56 SYSTEM057

The performance overhead of an event occurring shall be included in the resource budget when sizing the hardware platform.

57 SYSTEM058

The performance overhead of an event being retained shall be included in the resource budget when sizing the hardware platform.

58 SYSTEM059

The performance overhead of an event being reported to interested event consumers shall be included in the resource budget when sizing the hardware platform.

59 SYSTEM060

The performance overhead of an event occurring shall not impact the performance of required spacecraft behaviour.

60 SYSTEM061

The performance overhead of an event being retained shall not impact the performance of required spacecraft behaviour.

61 SYSTEM062

The performance overhead of an event being reported to interested event consumers shall not impact the performance of required spacecraft behaviour.

62 SYSTEM064

The method of specifying a value for an event shall not be burdensome for the flight software developers.

63 SYSTEM063

The method of specifying an event shall not be burdensome for the flight software developers.

64 SYSTEM065

The ETS shall not provide the ability to optionally compile down to zero-overhead through elimination of event occurrence, reporting and retention functionalities.

65 SYSTEM066

The ETS shall not maintain statistics about event reporting beyond that necessary to track whether an event still needs to be retained in accordance with registered event retention policies.

66 SYSTEM013
